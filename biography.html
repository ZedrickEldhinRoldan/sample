"Living Through Ups and Downs"

CHILDHOOD YEARS

     1I was born in Narvacan, Ilocos Sur. 2I grew up in a poor family. 4My childhood was far from happy. 5As I had to face life's struggles at an early age. 6 When I was three, my mother went abroad, hoping to give us a better future. 7But I was too young to understand what was happening. 8Because of this, my grandfather returned from abroad to take care of me, and I couldn't help but feel anger toward my father for wasting all of my mother's hard work. 9In Grade 4, I was forced to move to Manila because of my dad's actions. 10Adjusting to a new school and new classmates was incredibly difficult. 11The following year, I returned to Narvacan, thinking that my dad had finally changed, and for a while, it seemed like he did. 12But as time passed, he fell back into his old habits. 13Once again, our family became the talk of the town. 14The judgment and gossip hurt, but one day, my cousins invited me to church, a place that would soon become my refuge. 15At first, I didn't understand anything, but I saw the joy in people's faces, and slowly, I started to feel it too. 16I learned how to play the drums and found comfort in new friendships, yet my family's problems never truly went away. 17In Grade 6, I fell seriously ill, and at my weakest moment, my father remained distant, showing me no care or concern. 18It was this painful reality that pushed my mother to finally come home. 19Unable to bear the thought of me suffering alone. 20Though my family was still broken, her return brought me a sense of warmth and love that I had longed for.

TEENAGE YEARS

     1When I turned 13, I started high school, and school became my safe place. 2I made new friends, and being with them made me really happy. 3Unlike home, where everything felt heavy, school was full of fun and laughter. 4My friends brought joy into my life, but every time I went home, I felt sad again. 5To escape, I spent more time at church and became part of the music ministry. 6I prayed every night, hoping that one day, my family would come with me and change for the better. 7After some time, I convinced my mom to join, and to my surprise, she didn't just attend, she became one of the lead singers. 8My dad, however, stayed the same, lost in his bad habits. 9When my older brother was about to enter college, my mom had to return abroad, leaving me, my dad, and my grandfather at home. 10Life became even harder when we found out that my dad was drowning in debt, and with my brother's school expenses, we didn't know how to survive. 11Soon after, my parents separated, and everything felt like it was falling apart. 12Because of this, I stopped going to church and only focused on school. 13I was happy at school, but once I got home, I felt down again. 14I envied my friends because their families were complete and happy, while mine was broken. 15Then, when I turned 16, something unexpected happened, my dad apologized. 16Even though he had hurt us, we forgave him because he was still our father. 17Later on, my parents got back together, and a year aftermthat, my mom came back home. 18That was also when I returned to church, but my dad still refused to come. 19At first, I thought he would never change, but after a few months, he finally joined us. 20The biggest surprise was that, over time, he became the one waking us up every Sunday for church, that was the happiest moment of my life because I finally saw how much things had changed, and I knew it was a blessing.

COLLEGE STORY

     1I grew up in the province. 2 Adjusting to life in the city felt impossible, and my first week in the dorm was the loneliest time of my life. 3I cried a lot because I missed my family and my old friends. 4During my first semester, I had no friends at all, so I had to rely on myself for everything. 5I wanted to go home so badly because I felt so alone. 6But in my second semester, everything changed, I finally found friends, and I was so thankful that they were good people with kind hearts. 7Unlike others who got into bad company, I was lucky to be surrounded by friends who truly cared. 8They became my constant companions, and we shared many great moments together. 9However, college was still stressful, and sometimes, the workload became too much. 10There were times when I thought about transferring schools because of how overwhelmed I felt. 11I also had to work hard because I felt a lot of pressure from my parents, who had high expectations of me. 12But my friends were always there for me, comforting and encouraging me to keep going. 13They were different from my old friends, and I knew I could always count on them. 14As a probinsyano, living alone in the city was a big challenge, and it took me months to adjust. 15Here, I also learned how to save money and budget wisely. 16Thankfully, I had real friends who made everything easier. 17The happiness I felt when I was with them was priceless, and I knew I could trust them in anything. 18They became one of the biggest blessings in my life, giving me the strength to keep fighting. 19No matter what I was going through, I never heard a single word of anger from them, only love and understanding, studying while being far from home was never easy, and there were so many struggles I had to overcome. 20But I learned that having the right people around me made a huge difference, my friends may not be able to help me financially, but their kindness and support were worth more than anything money could buy.
